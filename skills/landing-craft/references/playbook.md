# Landing Conversion Playbook (load before copy/structure)

A high-converting landing is a guided argument: grab attention → make it relevant → prove it →
remove doubt → ask for the action. This is the architecture. Adapt the sections to the offer —
but never lose the *job* each one does.

## The 5-second test (the only test that matters at the top)

A stranger landing cold must answer, from the hero alone, in five seconds:
**What is it? · Who is it for? · Why is it better? · What do I do next?**
If the hero doesn't answer all four, nothing below matters. Write the hero last-ish, but design
the whole page to make the hero's promise true.

## Section sequence (default order — cut what doesn't apply)

| # | Section | Its job | Notes |
|---|---------|---------|-------|
| 1 | **Hero** | Promise + primary CTA in 5s | Headline = outcome, not feature. Sub = how/for-whom. One CTA. |
| 2 | **Social proof strip** | Borrowed trust, instantly | Logos / "X teams" / a number. Right under the hero. |
| 3 | **Problem / stakes** | Make them feel the pain | Name the status quo they're stuck with. |
| 4 | **Solution / value** | How you remove the pain | 3–5 outcomes, not a feature dump. Show, don't list. |
| 5 | **How it works** | Reduce perceived effort | 3 steps max. "Sandbox in 60s." |
| 6 | **Proof / results** | Believe the promise | Testimonials with faces+names, metrics, case studies. |
| 7 | **Objection handling** | Disarm the "but…" | FAQ, comparison, guarantee, "no credit card". |
| 8 | **Offer / pricing** | Make the decision easy | Anchor, clear tiers, the free path first if there is one. |
| 9 | **Final CTA** | Ask again, decisively | Restate the core promise; one button. |

## The hero formula

```
[Headline]  →  the OUTCOME the user gets (specific, not adjectives)
[Subhead]   →  how it works + who it's for, one sentence
[Primary CTA] → the one action (verb + low friction): "Empezar gratis"
[Trust line] → removes the immediate risk: "Gratis hasta 100/mes. Sin tarjeta."
[Visual]    → a real product shot / demo / code, never a generic illustration
```
Headline patterns that work: *outcome* ("Cut your month-end close from 5 days to 2 hours"),
*before→after*, *capability* ("Cobrá en 30 países con una línea de código"). Avoid: "La mejor
plataforma de…", "Revoluciona tu…", "Solución todo-en-uno".

## CTA rules

- **One primary action**, repeated 3–4× down the page (hero, after value, after proof, final).
- Everything else is secondary (ghost/link). Two equal CTAs = zero CTAs.
- Verbs of progress, not commitment: "Empezar gratis" / "Ver la demo" > "Comprar ahora" up top.
- Reduce friction at the button: state the cost of clicking ("Sin tarjeta", "60s").

## Proof patterns (in order of power)

Specific numbers > named testimonials with faces > logos > star ratings > vague claims.
Always attribute (name, role, company, photo). One concrete metric ("redujo el cierre de mes de
5 días a 2 horas") beats a paragraph of praise.

## Copy principles (anti-slop)

- **Speak to one person**, in their words, about their problem — not about you.
- **Specifics over adjectives.** Numbers, names, timeframes, concrete nouns.
- **Cut hedge words** (just, very, really, simply) and AI tells ("seamless", "elevate",
  "unlock", "in today's fast-paced world", "game-changer").
- **One idea per section.** If a sentence does two jobs, split it.
- Read it aloud. If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite it like you're explaining it to a friend.

## Intuitive UX checklist

- One obvious primary action per viewport. The eye should never hunt.
- Visual hierarchy = reading order. Size/weight/colour guide, they don't decorate.
- Tap targets ≥ 44px; forms ask the minimum; labels visible; errors inline and kind.
- Mobile-first: the hero promise + CTA must land above the fold on a 390px screen.
- Nothing moves that the user didn't cause, except the one hero reveal.

## Conversion heuristics the review phase scores

1. 5-second test passes on the hero alone.
2. Exactly one primary CTA identity, repeated.
3. At least one piece of concrete proof above the mid-point.
4. An objection/risk is explicitly handled.
5. Mobile hero shows promise + CTA without scrolling.
6. No AI-slop tells in copy or layout.
7. AA contrast, visible focus, reduced-motion honored.
8. LCP element is the hero, not a heavy below-fold asset.
