# Market Research method (the landing-research agent's playbook)

A landing is a product in a market. This is how to study that market so the result is *current and
specific*, not generic. Be thorough — tokens are not the constraint; a shallow study yields a
generic page. Use real data only (Firecrawl/WebSearch); never fabricate numbers, competitors, or
quotes.

## 1. Product & idea validation
Scrape the product/site (Firecrawl). Capture: category, what it really does, the offer, real proof.
Then a one-line **idea check**: is the promise credible and is there a buyer who feels this pain?

## 2. Competitor teardown (3–5)
Find real competitors (WebSearch "<category> alternatives / vs / best"). Scrape each. Fill:

| Competitor | Angle / hero promise | Section structure | Proof used | Pricing posture | Visual vibe |
|------------|----------------------|-------------------|------------|-----------------|-------------|

Then name **the GAP**: the positioning/angle no competitor owns that fits our product. That gap is
the strategy's starting point.

## 3. Keyword & search-intent research
List **primary** (head) + **long-tail** keywords the audience searches, each tagged by intent
(informational / commercial / transactional). Note what competitors rank for and the language they
use. These become: the page→keyword map (per `site-architecture.md`), the SEO targets, and the
*words in the copy*. Lean on `seo-geo`.

## 4. Audience & emotional drivers
- **Who** buys (segment, role, context).
- **JTBD** — the job they hire this for.
- **Emotional drivers** — what they FEEL and want, in THEIR words (status, fear, relief, ambition).
  Pull real phrasing from reviews/forums/competitor testimonials where possible. This is the raw
  material that makes copy human instead of a feature list.

## 5. Alive design references (3–5)
Search "best <niche> landing page" + use any examples the user gave. For each reference, score with
`alive-not-generic.md`: real imagery? signature visual? scroll-reactive motion? warmth/colour?
unique sections? Capture the ONE move worth borrowing (not copying). These steer the design phase so
the result fits the THEME, not a template.

## 6. Synthesis → the research verdict
Three lines the rest of the pipeline runs on:
1. **Angle** — the positioning we'll lead with (from the gap).
2. **Gap we own** — what no competitor says that we can.
3. **Emotional hook** — the feeling the copy leads with.
Plus the **proposed unique sections** for this theme.

## Output
`landing/research.md` — sections 1–6 above, concrete and sourced. This artifact is READ by strategy,
architecture, copy, design and SEO. If a fact can't be found, say so; don't invent it.
